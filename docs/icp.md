## Decided ICP hierarchy

| Role | ICP tier | Description |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Department chair / program director | **Primary buyer** | Holds curriculum budget; owns accreditation accountability; signs vendor contracts |
| Faculty / instructor | Secondary user | Configures assignments; reviews ledger; defends individual grades |
| Students | End user | Receives concept checks; completes verification interactions |

**Sales motion implication:** The door opens through a department chair or program director,
not an individual faculty member. Faculty may champion internally, but the contract
signer and budget holder is the chair/director.

## Decided beachhead segment

**Segment:** Professional graduate programs, especially online-heavy programs in:
- Nursing (clinical reasoning, licensing board accountability)
- Law (legal analysis, bar exam stakes)
- MBA (applied case analysis, professional credentialing)
- Cybersecurity (hands-on technical verification, certification alignment)
- Data / AI programs (code/analysis verification, portfolio evidence)
- Other high-stakes applied fields with professional licensing or accreditation pressure

**Why this segment first:**
- Highest grade-appeal pain — professional licensing boards demand evidence of competency
- Online delivery creates maximum verification pressure (no in-person proctoring option)
- Program director has budget closer to curriculum level, not IT procurement
- Accreditation pressure is program-specific, not institution-wide (faster pilot path)
- High ACV potential: smaller programs, higher stakes, premium pricing tolerance

**Demo scenario anchor:** Use a high-stakes professional graduate assignment for all demos.
Examples: clinical case analysis (nursing), legal brief (law), security incident response (cybersec).
Program chair persona — not individual faculty, not provost.

## Buyer pain profile (primary buyer)

**Department chair / program director pain:**
- Responsible for accreditation outcomes but lacks evidence of per-student learning verification
- Grade appeals land on their desk — they have no audit trail, only the instructor's recollection
- AI policy pressure from institution, faculty union, and students simultaneously
- Professional licensing boards are starting to ask for competency documentation
- Online program growth means less visibility into how students are actually learning

**What Acta solves for this buyer:**
- Ledger gives them a defensible record when a grade is appealed up the chain
- Accreditation export produces the evidence documentation accreditors are beginning to require
- Grading mode selector lets them set policy at the program level, not leave it to each faculty member
- Signed provenance means they can answer "how do you know students understood this?" with a document
